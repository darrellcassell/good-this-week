Two or three cups of coffee a day was linked to better mental health in a 460,000-person study.

Researchers tracked participants for over thirteen years. The middle held up. The light drinkers and heavy drinkers missed out.

United Kingdom · By Darrell C. · 3 min read · April 19, 2026
— Photograph would go here —
A cup of espresso, early morning light, on a kitchen table

461,586 people. Thirteen and a half years. The curve was shaped like a J.

For thirteen and a half years, researchers at the UK Biobank watched what 461,586 people drank in the morning, and then watched what happened to them.

Specifically, they watched for mood disorders — depression, bipolar — and for stress disorders, the PTSD-and-acute-stress kind. None of the participants had been diagnosed with anything when the study began. They were just people aged 40 to 69, drinking their coffee, going about their lives.

The pattern, when it emerged, was J-shaped. Two or three cups a day was the floor of the curve. Drink less than that, and you didn't see the benefit. Drink more, and the protection started to fade. The middle held up.

The protective association showed up regardless of whether the coffee was instant, ground, or decaffeinated, which is the kind of finding that complicates the obvious explanation. If decaf is helping too, it's not just the caffeine doing the work. Researchers don't know what is.

The association was stronger in men, for reasons the paper doesn't try to settle.

Studies like this don't prove that coffee causes anything. They show what showed up next to what, across a very large group of people, over a very long period of time. People who drink moderate amounts of coffee tend to also do other things — sleep on a schedule, leave the house, sit with other humans in a kitchen — that can't be cleanly separated from the coffee itself.

Still. Of all the things that have been linked to better outcomes for the brain over the years, the morning cup keeps coming up. The middle of the curve, the people doing the boring, regular thing, doing it twice.

Source: Euronews, "Two to three cups of coffee a day linked to lower risk of mental health disorders, study finds," April 15, 2026; UK Biobank cohort study.

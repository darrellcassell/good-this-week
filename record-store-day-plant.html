Record Store Day turned 19 on Saturday, and Robert Plant got named a Legend.

Thousands of independent record shops opened early. Plant got a permanent plaque at the world's oldest record store, in Cardiff.

Cardiff, Wales · By Darrell C. · 3 min read · April 19, 2026
— Photograph would go here —
Robert Plant at Spillers Records in Cardiff, holding an EP, smiling

Spillers Records in Cardiff is the world's oldest record shop. Plant is the third Record Store Day Legend, after Elton John and Johnny Marr.

Record Store Day is nineteen years old now. On Saturday morning, April 18, thousands of independent record stores around the world opened their doors early, mostly for the same group of people who have been doing this every year since the first one in 2008: collectors, completists, and the kind of music fan who still believes the cover matters.

The advice from one veteran behind the counter, repeated in basically every interview with a record-shop owner this week: bring a list. By lunchtime, the rare pressings are gone.

This year's slate ran to over 350 exclusive titles, organized by genre. Taylor Swift had a 7-inch. Bruno Mars, who served as the year's official ambassador, released a compilation of his collaborations. The Grateful Dead put out a five-LP set from a 1976 show. Slipknot finally released Look Outside Your Window, an album they recorded in 2008.

The bigger honor was the Record Store Legend award, which goes out rarely. Robert Plant got it. He is the third recipient, after Elton John in 2017 and Johnny Marr in 2025. A plaque was unveiled at Spillers Records in Cardiff, the world's oldest record shop, where Plant was photographed holding a copy of his own EP and looking, by all accounts, genuinely pleased.

"Record stores have always been a part of my life. For me, once you get to the physical record it's because you really want to know and be a part of what the artist was considering."

— Robert Plant

U.S. vinyl revenue passed $1.04 billion in 2025 for the first time since 1983. The format that was supposed to die kept not dying.

Source: Billboard, April 16, 2026; Record Store Day UK press release; RIAA mid-year vinyl report.
